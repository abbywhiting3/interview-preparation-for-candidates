General Attorney Questions

Common questions asked in attorney interviews across practice areas.

Why did you choose to practice law in this specific area? ▼

Connect your answer to a genuine interest or formative experience. Reference specific cases, coursework, or mentors that drew you to the practice area.

Tip: Avoid generic answers. Mention a specific moment or experience that ignited your passion.
How do you manage a heavy caseload and competing deadlines? ▼

Describe your organizational system: calendaring tools, task prioritization, and how you communicate with partners about bandwidth.

Tip: Mention specific tools like practice management software or docketing systems.
Tell me about a difficult opposing counsel situation and how you handled it. ▼

Use the STAR method. Focus on professionalism, de-escalation, and keeping the client's best interest front and center.

Tip: Never badmouth opposing counsel. Frame the difficulty around the situation, not the person.
What is your approach to client development and business generation? ▼

Discuss networking, industry involvement, writing or speaking engagements, and maintaining referral relationships.

Tip: Even junior attorneys should show awareness of business development through bar associations, CLEs, or community involvement.
Describe a time you made an error on a case. What happened? ▼

Be honest but strategic. Choose a real but recoverable mistake. Walk through what happened, how you addressed it, and safeguards you put in place.

Tip: Firms value self-awareness and accountability.
Why are you looking to leave your current firm? ▼

Frame your answer around what you are moving toward, not what you are running from. Talk about growth, practice area alignment, or culture fit.

Tip: Never speak negatively about your current employer.

Insurance Defense Questions

Questions commonly asked when interviewing for insurance defense attorney roles.

What is your experience defending insurance carriers and their insureds? ▼

Detail the types of carriers, lines of coverage (auto, CGL, professional liability), and your role from coverage analysis through trial.

Tip: Name specific carrier clients if allowed. Hiring partners want to gauge alignment with their book.
How do you handle billing guidelines and litigation management requirements? ▼

Discuss familiarity with carrier billing guidelines, rate caps, task-based billing codes, and litigation plans.

Tip: Carriers care about cost-efficient defense, not just legal wins. Show you understand the business side.
Describe your experience with coverage analysis and reservation of rights letters. ▼

Walk through your process for reviewing a policy, identifying coverage issues, and communicating with the carrier.

Tip: If you have experience with both coverage opinions and defense work, highlight that dual perspective.
How do you evaluate a case for early resolution vs. trial? ▼

Explain your framework: liability exposure, damages assessment, litigation costs, policy limits, and the insured's risk appetite.

Tip: Demonstrate strategic thinking about total cost of defense.
Tell me about your deposition and trial experience. ▼

Quantify: depositions taken/defended, mediations, trials. Describe your approach to witness prep and cross-examination.

Tip: Be specific with numbers. 50+ depositions is more impactful than "significant experience."

Real Estate Attorney Questions

Questions for commercial and transactional real estate attorney roles.

What types of real estate transactions have you handled? ▼

Break down by type: commercial acquisitions, leasing, financing, development, residential closings. Mention deal sizes.

Tip: Quantify your deals. $50M+ in transactions carries weight.
How do you approach due diligence on a commercial deal? ▼

Walk through: title review, survey analysis, environmental reports, zoning, lease abstracts, estoppels, entity formation.

Tip: Show you are systematic. Missed due diligence issues can kill deals.
Describe a deal that hit a major obstacle. How did you handle it? ▼

Use STAR. Describe the issue (title defect, environmental, financing failure) and how you found solutions.

Tip: Partners want attorneys who find solutions, not just flag problems.
What is your experience with commercial lease negotiations? ▼

Describe landlord vs. tenant representation, key provisions: rent escalations, CAM, assignment, renewal options, TI allowances.

Tip: If you have represented both sides, highlight the strategic advantage.

Workers' Compensation Questions

Common questions for workers' compensation attorney interviews.

Do you primarily represent claimants, employers, or carriers? ▼

Clearly state your side and typical caseload. If dual-side experience, discuss how it gives insight into opposing strategies.

Tip: Workers' comp firms are often side-specific. Be clear about your experience.
How do you handle a disputed claim where the employer denies the injury is work-related? ▼

Describe evidence gathering: medical records, witness statements, job descriptions, surveillance, IME coordination.

Tip: Show your knowledge of the administrative hearing process.
What is your experience with Medicare Set-Asides and settlement structures? ▼

Discuss MSA allocations, CMS submissions, and structured settlements.

Tip: MSA knowledge is increasingly valuable. Highlight direct CMS experience.
Describe your hearing experience before the workers' comp board. ▼

Quantify appearances. Describe hearing types: TTD, PPD, vocational rehab disputes, settlement approvals.

Tip: Workers' comp is a volume practice. High hearing counts show you can handle the pace.

Family Law Questions

Questions for family law attorney interviews.

How do you handle high-conflict custody disputes? ▼

Discuss managing emotional clients, working with guardians ad litem, and presenting best-interests evidence.

Tip: Demonstrate emotional intelligence. Firms want attorneys who manage emotions while staying strategic.
What is your experience with complex asset division? ▼

Detail experience with business valuations, QDROs, stock options, hidden asset investigations, and forensic accountants.

Tip: High-net-worth work requires financial literacy. Show you can work with numbers.
How do you approach settlement negotiations in family law? ▼

Explain mediation experience, collaborative law training, and how you prepare clients for realistic outcomes.

Tip: Courts favor settlement. Show you are a skilled negotiator, not just a litigator.
Describe a case involving an order of protection. ▼

Walk through emergency petitions, evidentiary hearings, witness prep, and impact on related proceedings.

Tip: These cases involve safety. Show you take them seriously and act quickly.

Corporate / Transactional Questions

Questions for corporate and transactional attorney roles.

What types of transactions have you worked on? ▼

Detail M&A, private equity, venture capital, or general corporate experience. Specify deal sizes and your level of responsibility.

Tip: Be clear: did you draft the purchase agreement or review ancillary documents? Honesty builds credibility.
How do you manage multiple closings simultaneously? ▼

Describe project management: closing checklists, signature pages, escrow coordination, and multi-party communication.

Tip: Transactional work is about execution. Show you are organized and calm under pressure.
Walk me through negotiating a key provision in a purchase agreement. ▼

Pick one you know well (indemnification, reps and warranties, earnouts) and explain your approach to risk allocation.

Tip: This tests technical depth. Pick something you have actually negotiated.

Paralegal Questions

Common interview questions for paralegal candidates.

What types of cases have you supported and what was your role? ▼

Walk through your experience: drafting discovery, managing document review, coordinating depositions, filing motions, maintaining case calendars.

Tip: Be specific about volume. Managing a 50,000-document production is more impactful than "document review."
How do you prioritize tasks when multiple attorneys need help simultaneously? ▼

Explain your approach: checking deadlines, distinguishing court dates from internal deadlines, keeping attorneys informed about bandwidth.

Tip: Show you are proactive about managing expectations.
What legal software and technology are you proficient in? ▼

List platforms: Westlaw, LexisNexis, Relativity, NetDocuments, Clio, iManage. Mention e-filing and certifications.

Tip: If unfamiliar with a specific tool, emphasize how quickly you learn with a concrete example.
Describe a time you caught an error that could have affected a case. ▼

Use STAR. Describe what you found, the potential impact, how you flagged it, and the result.

Tip: Paralegals are the last line of defense on details. This story should prove it.

Legal Assistant Questions

Interview questions for legal assistant roles.

What administrative and legal support tasks are you most experienced with? ▼

Cover calendar management, correspondence drafting, filing (electronic and physical), client intake, and expense processing.

Tip: Show range. Legal assistants who handle both admin and substantive tasks are highly valued.
How do you handle confidential client information? ▼

Explain your understanding of attorney-client privilege, secure document handling, and your habits around data security.

Tip: Mention specific practices: locking workstations, secure shredding, encrypted email.
Describe a time you had to manage an urgent, last-minute request from an attorney. ▼

Use STAR. Show how you stayed calm, reprioritized, communicated with affected parties, and delivered.

Tip: Legal work is deadline-driven. Show you thrive under pressure.

Legal Secretary Questions

Interview questions for legal secretary positions.

What document management systems and legal formatting standards are you familiar with? ▼

List systems (iManage, NetDocuments, Worldox) and formatting knowledge (Table of Authorities, Tables of Contents, blue-backing, court filing standards).

Tip: Formatting precision matters in legal. Show you know court-specific requirements.
How do you manage calendars and deadlines for multiple attorneys? ▼

Describe your docketing approach, reminder systems, and how you confirm upcoming deadlines with attorneys proactively.

Tip: A missed deadline can end a case. Show you have multiple safeguards in place.
Tell me about a time you had to coordinate a complex filing under a tight deadline. ▼

Use STAR. Cover the filing requirements, the time pressure, how you coordinated with attorneys, and the result.

Tip: E-filing failures happen. Show you have backup plans and verify confirmations.

General Finance Questions

Common interview questions for financial services professionals.

How do you approach risk assessment in financial transactions? ▼

Explain your process: data review, stakeholder consultation, scenario modeling, and communicating findings.

Tip: Balance thoroughness with business practicality. Firms want risk-aware, not risk-averse.
Describe a time you identified a discrepancy in financial data. ▼

Use STAR. What you found, potential impact, how you investigated, and what controls you recommended.

Tip: Attention to detail is everything in finance. Prove it with a real story.
How do you stay current with financial regulations and market conditions? ▼

Mention publications, professional associations, continuing education, and how you translate changes into action.

Tip: Show you are a self-directed learner who does not wait to be told about changes.

Compliance Questions

Interview questions for compliance roles in financial services.

Walk me through your experience with regulatory compliance programs. ▼

Discuss specific regulations: SEC, FINRA, SOX, AML/KYC. Describe your role in audits, reporting, and policy implementation.

Tip: Reference regulations by name to show depth.
How do you handle a situation where you discover a compliance violation? ▼

Walk through your escalation process, documentation, remediation plan, and how you communicated with leadership.

Tip: Show that you prioritize the organization's integrity while being solutions-oriented.
How do you balance business objectives with compliance requirements? ▼

Explain how you work with business teams to find compliant paths forward rather than simply saying no.

Tip: The best compliance professionals are enablers, not blockers. Frame yourself that way.

Accounting Questions

Interview questions for accounting and financial reporting roles.

Walk me through your month-end close process. ▼

Describe your checklist: journal entries, accruals, reconciliations, variance analysis, and reporting timelines.

Tip: Show efficiency and accuracy. Mention how you shortened close timelines or improved accuracy.
What accounting software and ERP systems are you experienced with? ▼

List platforms: QuickBooks, Sage, NetSuite, SAP, Oracle. Mention advanced Excel skills and any automation you have built.

Tip: If you have built automated reports or streamlined processes, that differentiates you.
Describe a time you found and corrected an accounting error. ▼

Use STAR. Describe the error, how you found it, the financial impact, and the controls you implemented to prevent recurrence.

Tip: This is your chance to demonstrate the precision and integrity that define strong accountants.

The STAR Method

A proven framework for answering behavioral interview questions with clarity and impact.

S

Situation

Set the scene. Describe the context or challenge you faced.

T

Task

Explain your specific responsibility or objective.

A

Action

Detail the steps you personally took.

R

Result

Share the outcome. Quantify it if possible.

Example: Attorney Using STAR

S: A high-value client threatened to leave after a billing dispute during complex commercial litigation.
T: As the associate managing the account, I needed to resolve the dispute and retain the client.
A: I reviewed every line item with the client, prepared a revised summary, coordinated with the partner on a fair adjustment, and created a billing communication protocol.
R: The client stayed, approved the invoice within 48 hours, and expanded our engagement by two matters worth $200K+ annually.

Rehearse STAR stories. Saying them aloud builds confidence and reduces anxiety.
